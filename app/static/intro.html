ChromoLab

Bring annotation, AI training, validation, and research direction into one experimental control plane.

ChromoLab uses chromosome imagery as the example domain, but the platform is built for a larger mission: a durable AI research workflow where data intake, online annotation, model comparison, continuous optimization, validation review, and the next research hypothesis all live in one operational loop.

Positioning Not a one-off demo, but an AI research operations system that can keep learning with the team.

Every new batch, every revision, and every failed case becomes part of the evidence that drives the next experiment.

Example Domain Chromosomes, cells, pathology, and microscopy workflows

The current demonstration starts with chromosome segmentation and abnormality analysis, but the platform is meant for any visual research program that needs annotation and AI training in a closed loop.

Core Rhythm Data arrives, labeling advances, models compete, results are reviewed, research moves again

The point is not only to run models, but to manage the full cadence of data quality, experimentation, and research direction.

Capabilities

Bring the work that usually lives across disconnected tools into one operating flow.

Batch Dataset Intake

Upload YOLO-format ZIP datasets and let the platform normalize train and validation structure, image counts, label coverage, and class distribution.

Online Annotation Ops

Annotators can edit segmentation polygons in the browser while admins turn annotation progress into the next training revision.

Training Orchestration

Admins can schedule deliberate model comparisons or let the optimizer keep exploring new revisions and training settings.

Validation Review

Every training run keeps metrics, previews, and logs so the team can see whether progress came from data, configuration, or model choice.

Research Direction Guidance

The system does more than report scores. It points toward the next experiments based on coverage gaps, imbalance, revisions, and completed runs.

Role-Based Back Office

Separate admin, annotator, and viewer responsibilities so data governance, labeling, and review do not collapse into the same role.

Operational Rhythm

Think of it as an AI production line designed for research instead of pure delivery.

01

Ingest Data

Researchers bring in new image batches or prepared datasets and immediately see how ready the data really is.

02

Advance Annotation

Annotators close the gaps online and move the dataset forward, making each revision a formal experimental unit.

03

Run Comparisons

Admins choose model combinations and training conditions, or hand the revision to the optimizer for continuous scheduling.

04

Review and Redirect

Results, previews, and data gaps are turned back into new research directions instead of ending as a single metric on a chart.

Research Thesis

The goal is not only to train a model, but to help the team arrive at the next meaningful question faster.

From Annotation Toward Active Learning

The next step is to connect model-assisted pre-labeling, uncertainty-driven sampling, and human review into a tighter loop.

From Comparison Toward Validation Science

Do not only compare mAP. Keep hard cases stable, preserve revision-to-revision baselines, and ask why a model improved.

From a Single Use Case Toward a General Research Platform

Chromosomes are only the opening case. The same operating model can extend to cell analysis, pathology, microscopy, and lab imaging pipelines.
